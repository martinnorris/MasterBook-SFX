Skill.getCost.eq.1Necroscope

The basic power of a Necroscope is to talk to the dead. Make the SFX ESP:Necroscope skill roll against the SFX DN (Specific.getCostDN). If you SFX roll succeeds add the amount you exceed the roll to the effect value ()Effect.getValue), compare to the spirit Skill.getOpposition and check the Skill.getResult column for the result. There is a modifier for the result.

SituationModifier

Deceased is an acquaintance-1
Deceased just wants to talk-1
Deceased knows and doesn't like Necroscope+1
Deceased knows and hates Necroscope+3 or more

A dead spirit can also act as a teacher to the Necroscope once a connection has been created. To learn requires a teaching roll on part of the spirit plus a ESP:Necroscope skill roll. The difficulty of the SFX roll is the range value plus twice the number of skill adds to be learned.

The Necroscope can vector the skills of a dead person once contact has been made. The difference in the basis attribute for the skill between the Necroscope and the target is used to limit the number of skill adds that can be vectored and as a contribution to the DN. e.g. a Necroscope with INT 10 vectoring a spirit with INT 12 can only vector the target's skill adds + 10 - 12 The DN is double the number of skill adds to be vectored plus the difference between attribute levels.

The Necroscope can channel the complete skill set of a dead spirit allowing them to use the target skills and abilities. The difficulty of this is 10 + the ability and skill adds that are most appropriate

Skill.getCost.eq.3Prescient

You can see the future, sometimes flashes out of the blue, or sometimes when you make a subconscious connection. These both work in a similar way, but in the first case the Game Master tells you when to test your power because of a story critical moment, and in the second case you try to have a subconscious insight by association with words, images, touching something etc.,. The effect value (Effect.getValue) determines the time window you experience of Effect.getValue.getTime

When the Game Master feels a serious event could occur within the Prescient time window, the player makes an ESP:Prescient SFX roll DN (Specific.getCostDN), modified by the severity of the event. On success subtract the DN from the Prescient skill total, and look up this value as time for the length of the vision - 0 = 1 seconds; 9 = 1 minute; 18 = 1 hour etc.,. The length of the duration determines the level of detail the Prescient can extract from the vision

Free association is where the Prescient tries to trigger a vision by having someone recite words, or flash images, or even from touching something. The Prescient makes the SFX roll DN (Specific.getCostDN), and a success generates the duration of the vision from skill minus the DN. In this case the Prescient takes shock damage equal to the amount the Prescient beat the DN roll. For each test like this the DN increases by 1 until the Prescient fails a roll, and then they cannot try free association until the Game Master agrees. (Or optionally after the FV used as a delay)

Skill.getCost.eq.4Seer

A Seer can see the lifelines of living, sentient creatures. They perceive Humans and Wamphyrii life lines as different colours. In their perception of these lifelines the Seer sees how different lifelines have interacted in the past, and might interact in the future. The effect of the SFX gives the time (Effect.getValue.getTime) in the past or the future that the Seer can examine a lifelines. However a Seer can see multiple targets that could be the same person in the past and the future. To visualise interactions with other people the Seer needs an SFX that applies to multiple subjects.

The Seer makes the SFX skill roll ESP:Seer with DN (Specific.getCostDN) to determine how much (if anything) the Seer will reveal. For each point of the roll that beats the DN, the Seer learns 1 fact about the target When the lifeline of a person interacts with another unknown lifeline, the other lifeline can only be identified for certain is if the lifeline is for a person who is both within visual range and the range of the ESP power, plus within the effect range {time} of the SFX. The Seer can see the termination of a lifeline as it ceases, if that is within the time window of the SFX, and has some idea about the cause of death.

The lifeline of a person being 'reborn' as a Wamphyri has the termination associated with death, and then some time later the emergence of a fainter Wamphyri coloured lifeline.

Skill.getCost.eq.5Sensitive

Sensitives are ESP users who are attuned to a particular type of matter or energy, spiritual or tangible. The Sensitive SFX is used to determine the quantity and qualities of the target. The SFX is worked 'in reverse' using the attributes of the target to determine the effectiveness of the Sensitive. First roll the Sensitive's ESP:Sensitive skill against the SFX DN (Specific.getCostDN) and note the difference. Even negative values could provide information. The success level is then applied to a formula that includes the target mass and range. (For the mass and range use the equivalent value measure)

success = target mass - target range + SFX effect + difference noted above

Compare this value on the Skill.getResult column. A negative result here indicates that the Sensitive cannot detect the target. A success will give a general idea of quantity, range and bearing; while a spectacular success gives detailed information about the target. Range does affect the level of detail discovered.

Occasionally the Sensitive will subconsciously detect something that is too big to miss. In this case the Game Master will prompt the Sensitive if the source mass minus source range is more than 10

Skill.getCost.eq.6Shaman

A Shaman can cause actual physical harm to the target through spiritual means. The Shaman rolls ESP:Shaman with DN ()Specific.getCostDN). If this roll succeeds compare the effect (Effect.getValue) of the SFX against the appropriate target attribute and consult the Skill.getResult column. After this result the Shaman suffers shock, this is calculated as half the effect (Effect.getValue) minus the Shaman's confidence; and if the SFX roll exceeded double the effect reduced by SFX roll minus effect.

shock = (effect of SFX - CON)/2 - roll > (2 * effect of SFX) ? (effect of SFX - roll) : 0

A Shaman may use the SFX as long as they stay conscious, but these should be at different targets. Use the push rules instead of making several SFX on the same target but this is more dangerous. If the accumulated shock from using the SFX is greater than the Shaman's shock limit, the excess shock is converted to wounds

The damage caused by the Shaman is not typical physical damage, but is expressed as numerous minor injures or psychosomatic signs. The target of a Shaman's attack is fully aware of the source of the attack even if they do understand the nature.

Skill.getCost.eq.7Spotter

A Spotter can detect if another person has an ESP power. The power is 'on' all the time, so the Game Master can ask the Spotter to make a test for anyone they meet. The SFX is rolled using the characters ESP:Spotter skill with DN (Specific.getCostDN) with a bonus of the DN minus the range value. {This makes it harder to spot ESP users further away} If the Spotter succeeds on the roll, take the effect (Effect.getValue) minus the range value and compare the result on the Skill.getResult column. A minimal success identifies the person as an ESP user and recognisable as such in the future like a psychic fingerprint A spectacular success reveals the particular ESP power, their ESP skill level and SFX effect value.

A Spotter will also spot the taint of Wamphyri {even if they do not have a ESP power}. Use the Wamphyri willpower minus the range, instead of the effect minus range, for the bonus to the ESP:Spotter roll.

Skill.getCost.eq.8Telepath

Using their ESP:Telepath skill a Telepath can directly interrogate a subjects mind without any interference from lies and dissemination. A Telepath needs to know exactly where the target is located {from sight of the target or specific knowledge} or have an area or volume of effect factor in the SFX. Make the SFX ESP:Telepath skill roll against the DN Specific.getCostDN. The number by which the skill roll exceeded the DN is used as a bonus for the Telepath's interrogation attempts. If the skill roll is more than twice the DN (Specific.getCostDN.mul.2U) then add anything more than twice the DN as a modifier to the interrogation

The Telepath then has the effect (Effect.getValue) as time of Effect.getValue.getTime to interrogate the target. For each question the Telepath rolls her ESP:Telepath skill check with the bonus above. Add any modifier if the initial contact attempt was very successful. Compare this result to the value in the paranormal interrogation table for the type of information required. Move the value by the difference of the roll so success reduces the value and the time taken, while failure increases the value and time taken. The information gained is always truthful. When time begins to run out then the Telepath can push using normal push rules. While interrogating a target the Telepath can often accidentally pick up other surface thoughts of the person as the Game Master requires.

A Witch uses sympathetic magic to affect her or his self. This is a psychoactive application that modifies an attribute or skill or property of the Witch.

The SFX needs to be focused so that once it affects the witch the effect is maintained. Generally a component is required that is relevant to the sympathetic effect required.
